# Overview
Throughout the analysis period, the company generated a total revenue of 105,401,435.75 from 6,000,185 units sold, with an average order value (AOV) of 105.40. Revenue was largely driven by high-performing products such as Red Bull 12oz, K Cups Daily Chef Columbian Supremo, K Cups Original Donut Shop Medium Roast, K Cups Dunkin Donuts Medium Roast, and Muscle Milk Protein Shake Vanilla 11oz. These products consistently contributed the highest revenue and should be prioritized in inventory planning to minimize stockouts.

Revenue reached its highest level in 2018, suggesting that the business should investigate the factors that contributed to this exceptional performance—such as promotional campaigns, pricing strategies, or market conditions—to determine whether they can be replicated.

Additionally, the analysis shows that several premium-priced products are also among the highest revenue generators. This indicates strong customer demand for these products and suggests that the company can continue investing in premium product lines while ensuring consistent availability.

Further Insights
# Insight 1: The business depends heavily on a small group of products
Although the company sells hundreds of products, only a handful of items—including Red Bull 12oz, K Cups Daily Chef Columbian Supremo, K Cups Original Donut Shop Medium Roast, K Cups Dunkin Donuts Medium Roast, and Muscle Milk Protein Shake Vanilla 11oz—generate the highest revenue.

Recommendation
- Prioritize these products in inventory planning.
- Maintain higher safety stock levels.
- Consider using them in promotional bundles to increase sales of slower-moving products.

# Insight 2 : Revenue is geographically concentrated
Dhaka Division contributes significantly more revenue than any other division.

Recommendation
- Investigate why Dhaka performs so well.
- Replicate successful sales strategies in lower-performing divisions.
- Increase marketing investment in high-potential regions outside Dhaka.

# Insight 3 : Premium products sell well
Contrary to what many businesses expect, several premium-priced products are also among the highest revenue generators.

This suggests customers are willing to pay higher prices when they perceive sufficient value.

Recommendation
- Avoid unnecessary price reductions on premium products.
- Expand the premium product portfolio.
- Market premium products more aggressively.


# Insight 4: Revenue remained remarkably stable over time
Except for the partial data in 2021, yearly revenue fluctuates only slightly.

This indicates a relatively stable business rather than one experiencing rapid growth.

Recommendation
- Focus on identifying new growth opportunities.
- Introduce new products or expand into underserved regions.

# Insight 5: Card payments dominate the business 

Most transactions and most revenue come from card payments.

Recommendation
- Ensure card payment systems remain reliable.
- Negotiate better processing fees with banks.
- Continue encouraging digital payments.

# Insight 6: Average Order Value is consistent 

The Average Order Value remains around 105, regardless of payment method or region. 
This suggests customer purchasing behavior is fairly consistent across the business.

Recommendation

Increase AOV by introducing:
- product bundles,
- cross-selling,
- upselling,
- "Spend 150 and receive free shipping" campaigns.

# Insight 7: Several products deserve review 

Some products consistently generate very low revenue.

That does not necessarily mean they should be discontinued.

Possible reasons include:

- poor marketing,
- poor shelf placement,
- seasonal demand,
- niche customer base.
Recommendation
- Review these products individually before making discontinuation decisions.

# Insight 8: 2018 deserves investigation 

2018 produced the highest revenue in the dataset.
The data alone cannot explain why.
Possible factors include:
- successful promotions,
- introduction of popular products,
- pricing changes,
- market expansion,
- improved distribution.
Recommendation

- Compare 2018 with neighboring years to identify operational or marketing changes that can be replicated.

# Insight 9: The customer base appears diversified 
No single customer contributes a large share of total revenue.

This reduces dependence on a few major customers and lowers business risk.

Recommendation

- Continue customer acquisition while investing in loyalty programs to increase repeat purchases.

# Insight 10: Suppliers are not contributing equally 

A small number of suppliers account for a significant share of total revenue.

Recommendation
- Strengthen relationships with top-performing suppliers.
- Regularly evaluate supplier performance.
- Reduce reliance on any single supplier by diversifying where appropriate.

The business demonstrates stable sales performance, with strong demand for premium products and a geographically concentrated customer base led by Dhaka Division. Revenue is driven by a relatively small set of high-performing products and suppliers, while digital card payments dominate transactions. Future growth opportunities lie in expanding successful strategies to lower-performing regions, increasing average order value through targeted marketing, and optimizing the product portfolio by supporting top sellers and reviewing underperforming items.
